Nuclear ï¬ssion is a large-amplitude collective motion
mode involving up to hundreds of nucleons. Recently the
studies on nuclear ï¬ssion have been revived for its sig-
niï¬cance in both nuclear physics and astrophysics. For

of the ï¬ssion products [2â€“4]. Theoretically, statistical ï¬s-
sion has been described well by microscopical theories

ing a few recent studies, for example, by computing the
potential energy surface in the macroscopic-microscopic
model with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) for the
macroscopic part and the Yukawa-folded potential for
the microscopic energy corrections, the ï¬ssion fragment
mass distributions are predicted in a broad mass region
ranging from Thorium to the most superheavy elements,
showing rather good consistency with the data if avail-
able [14, 15]. Using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock
approach, the reactions 238U+40Ca near the Coulomb
barrier have been investigated in terms of the inï¬‚uences
of various ingredients in eï¬€ective nuclear interaction, in-
cluding incompressibility, symmetry energy, eï¬€ective nu-

When the excitation energy or the angular momentum
becomes much high, as achieved in heavy-ion reactions
well above the Coulomb barrier, the ï¬ssion barrier tends
to vanish. As a consequence, the ï¬ssion life-time becomes
shorter by a factor of 10 to 100 and the variance of the
mass asymmetry increases signiï¬cantly (the mass asym-
metry Î· of the two fragments can be larger than 0.6),
compared to the statistical ï¬ssion [17â€“21]. In addition,
the friction grows with the increasing of nuclear temper-
ature and the diï¬€usion enhance the ï¬ssion probability
[22â€“24]. So the process of fast ï¬ssion is more compli-
cated and the dynamic feature of the ï¬ssion process is
of signiï¬cance. Usually the transport models have been
successfully applied to describe the mass and total kinetic
energy distributions and the time scale of the ï¬ssion [25â€“
34]. Time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory can give an
excellent qualitative and quantitative description of fast
ï¬ssion starting with large deformation[35].

With the advent of advanced 4Ï€ detectors for heavy-
ion reactions at Fermi energies,
fast ï¬ssion, usually
termed as dynamic ï¬ssion because of its vanishing ï¬ssion
barrier and short time scale, has recently been investi-

from 25 to 50 MeV/u, the angular distribution of the ï¬s-
sion axis w.r.t. the separation direction of projectile-like

exhibits an alignment enhancement at very forward an-

ï¬ssion events in 80Kr +48 Ca at 35 MeV/u using part of
FAZIA setup [39, 40] and in 124Sn +64 Ni at 35 MeV/u
using CHIMERA setup [37, 41]. Besides, the isospin con-
tent of the emitted IMFs in the dynamic breakup of PLF
shows a slight evolution as a function of the rotating an-

dynamic ï¬ssion is also demonstrated by analysis of the

The topic of fast ï¬ssion with the simultaneous emission
of the particles deserves further investigations because
the ï¬ssioning system provides an appropriate laboratory
to probe the density behavior of nuclear symmetry en-
ergy, which is of great interest in both nuclear physics
and astrophysics [43], particularly after the discovery of
the neutron star merging event GW170817. According
to transport model simulations on Ar + Au reactions, the
occurrence of fast ï¬ssion of the heavy target-like fragment
(TLF) enhances the emission of the coalescence-invariant
neutrons (CIN) in comparison to that in the events with-
out ï¬ssion[44]. Such enhancement can be associated with
the presence of neck emission as shown experimentally
[38, 45]. Due to the enriched emission of neutrons, ei-
ther free or bounded in clusters, and to the accumula-
tion of the isospin eï¬€ect in the long-time process, the rel-
ative neutron richness of the emitted particles provides
an eï¬€ective probe to the symmetry energy. The experi-
mental attempt to extract the nuclear symmetry energy
parameter has been reported in inverse kinetic reactions
124Sn +64 Ni, where the dynamic ï¬ssion of the heavy PLF
has been experimentally investigated by measuring the
three bodies coincidence.
It has been found that the
intermediate-mass fragments emitted from the neck re-
gion are more neutron-rich. Comparison to stochastic
mean-ï¬eld (SMF) simulations favors symmetry energy
with the slope parameter L â‰ˆ 80 MeV [38]. Later in
Ar + Au reactions at 30 MeV/u, the angular evolution
of the neutron excess of the emitted light charged parti-
cles (LCPs) has been used to probe the symmetry energy,
yielding a softer symmetry energy L â‰ˆ 47 Â± 14 MeV at
95% conï¬dential level [46].

In this paper, we present the experimental results of
the fast ï¬ssion following Ar+Au reactions at 30 MeV/u
using the Compact Spectrometer for Heavy Ion Experi-

ï¬ssion fragments have been investigated, and the coinci-
dent light charged particles (LCPs) at midrapidity have
been measured. The angular evolution of the neutron

mentum transfer windows. The article is arranged as

B. Results for Light Charged Particles

In this subsection, we investigate the emission of light-

It is helpful
