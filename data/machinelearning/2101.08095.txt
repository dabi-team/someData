2.2 Eï¬€ects and Handlers

Eï¬€ects and handlers are a structured method of including

2.3 Frank

4 Conclusion
We have seen the implementation and evaluation of AD in
Frank via Frankâ€™s operation semantics and four handlers:
evaluate, diff, reverse, and reversec. While evaluate and diff
do eï¬€ectively no program transformations, reverse and reversec
build up ancillary programs via delimited continuations. The
eï¬€ects and handler style of Frank allowed us to compose
and nest our deï¬ned handlers, which is especially appar-
ent in the modular deï¬nition of reversec which delegates
all Smooth commands to reverse. It may also be possible to
integrate multi-stage programming by using the system of
Wang et al.. In conclusion, we have illustrated in Frank that
eï¬€ects and handlers are a good match for AD, and that ef-
fects and handlers can be seen as a form of program manip-
ulation.
