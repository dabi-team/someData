Abstract Events from alpha interactions on the surfaces of
germanium detectors are a major contribution to the back-
ground in germanium-based searches for neutrinoless double-
beta decay. Surface events are subject to charge trapping, af-
fecting their pulse shape and reconstructed energy. A study of
alpha events on the passivated end-plate of a segmented true-
coaxial n-type high-purity germanium detector is presented.
Charge trapping is analysed in detail and an existing pulse-
shape analysis technique to identify alpha events is veriï¬ed
with mirror pulses observed in the non-collecting channels of
the segmented test detector. The observed radial dependence
of charge trapping conï¬rms previous results. A dependence
of the probability of charge trapping on the crystal axes is
observed for the ï¬rst time. A ï¬rst model to describe charge
trapping eï¬€ects within the framework of the simulation soft-
ware SolidStateDetectors.jl is introduced. The inï¬‚uence of
metalisation on events from low-energy gamma interactions
close to the passivated surface is also presented.

High-purity germanium (HPGe) detectors are well suited to
search for physics beyond the Standard Model such as neutri-

The LEGEND experiment [1, 5] will search for 0ğœˆğ›½ğ›½
decay in 76Ge using HPGe detectors based on the experience
of the preceding experiments Gerda [4, 9] and Majorana

A study of alpha events on the passivated surface of

For the ï¬rst time, the dependence of charge trapping on
the orientation of the crystal axes is presented. First attempts

package SolidStateDetectors.jl [16] are also shown.

In general, all contacts of germanium detectors are com-
pletely coated with a thin metal layer, i.e. are fully metalised.
However, the segments of the detector Super-Siegfried were
originally not fully metalised but were only partially met-

where data were taken with partially metalised contacts were
compared to results from data taken after the segments be-
came fully metalised. The inï¬‚uence of the extent of the met-

7 Summary and outlook

Charge trapping was observed in alpha events on the pas-
sivated surface of the end-plate of an n-type true-coaxial
segmented HPGe detector. Such events occur with energies
in the region around 2 MeV where the signal for 0ğœˆğ›½ğ›½ decay
in 76Ge would appear. The trapping of holes and electrons
was clearly identiï¬ed by truncated mirror pulses observed
on the segmented mantle of the detector. This identiï¬cation
conï¬rmed that alpha events can be selected eï¬€ectively by
analysing the tail slope of the core pulses. Thus, the back-
ground due to alphas on the passivated surfaces can be ef-
ï¬ciently suppressed for detectors as used in the ï¬rst phase
of the LEGEND experiment currently under construction at
the Gran Sasso underground laboratory.

Radial scans of the detector top surface showed that
charge trapping is dominated by hole trapping for events at
small radii and by electron trapping for events at large radii
as expected from the length of the respective drift paths.
The trapping probability per Âµm/ns2 was found to be three
times larger for electrons than for holes for drifts directly
underneath the passivated top surface. A dependence of the
probability of charge trapping on the crystal axes has been
observed for the ï¬rst time in GALATEA. Near a fast axis,
, less net charge trapping is observed than near a slow
